a) Feature Summary

What Was Implemented

A complete anomaly exploration system that transforms inert galaxy anomalies into an interactive gameplay loop. Anomalies are now discoverable when fleets enter their system and investigable via a fleet-based investigation order that progresses over multiple ticks, granting resource rewards and lore fragments upon completion.

PROJECT.md Reference

This addresses Milestone 1: Galaxy Generation & Navigation (exploration content) and directly implements the "Exploration content (anomalies/quests)" feature from the morning report's improvement research (item #6, High Priority), inspired by Endless Space 2's curiosity system.

Why It Matters for Gameplay

  • Exploration incentive — Players now have a reason to send fleets into uncharted systems beyond simple fog-of-war reveals
  • Resource sink / reward loop — Addresses the morning report bug "Credits/minerals accumulate with nothing to spend on" by giving players meaningful resource injections from exploration rather than just passive income
  • Fleet role diversity — Sensor-equipped scouts investigate faster, creating meaningful ship design choices for exploration fleets
  • Species differentiation — Each species has affinities for specific anomaly types, reinforcing asymmetric play styles (e.g. Luminari excel at living nebulae, Ashkari at salvage)
  • Narrative depth — Each anomaly type has a unique lore fragment that hints at the galaxy's deep history and foreshadows the Devourer threat

b) Design Decisions

Fleet-Based Investigation (Not Colony-Based)

Investigation requires a fleet physically present in the anomaly's system. This was chosen over a colony-based approach because:

  • Anomalies are in systems, not on planets — thematically, a ship surveys an anomaly
  • Creates interesting risk/reward: the fleet is occupied and vulnerable during investigation
  • Encourages fleet diversity (scouts with sensors vs. warships)

Progress Lost on Fleet Departure

If the investigating fleet moves away or is destroyed, all progress is lost. This was considered against alternatives (pause progress, retain partial progress) and chosen because:

  • It creates meaningful strategic tension — do you defend the investigating fleet or let it finish?
  • It prevents exploiting investigation by pulsing fleets in and out
  • It's simple to understand and consistent with real-world survey operations

One Investigator Per Anomaly

Only one fleet can investigate a given anomaly at a time (first-come, first-served). This prevents race conditions and creates competitive exploration between empires.

JSON-Driven Rewards (Moddable)

All anomaly reward data is in anomaly-rewards.json, following the project's modding-first design pillar. Community modders can add new anomaly types or adjust rewards without touching engine code.

Sensor Component Bonus

Each sensor/advanced_sensors component in the fleet reduces investigation time by 5% (max 50%). Combined with the species research trait bonus (up to 30%), the theoretical maximum reduction is 70%, with a hard floor of 5 ticks. This rewards purposeful ship design without trivialising investigation.

Investigation Speed Formula

researchBonus = (research_trait - 1) / 9 * 0.30   // 0-30%
sensorBonus   = sensors_count * 0.05                // 0-50% (capped)
totalReduction = min(researchBonus + sensorBonus, 0.70)
finalTicks    = max(baseTicks * (1 - totalReduction), 5)

f) Integration Notes

System Interactions

  • Fleet movement — Anomaly discovery occurs automatically when fleets arrive in a system (step 1 of game loop)
  • Combat — If a fleet is attacked while investigating, the fleet may be destroyed, cancelling the investigation
  • AI decisions — investigate_anomaly is a new decision type with personality-specific weights, evaluated alongside all other AI priorities
  • Ship design — Sensor and advanced_sensors components now have a gameplay effect beyond combat (faster investigation)
  • Save/load — explorationOrders serialised as a plain array (no Map conversion needed); backward-compatible with saves that lack the field
  • Notifications — anomaly_investigated added as an info-priority auto-pause notification

Backward Compatibility

explorationOrders is optional (?:) on GameTickState and defaults to [] throughout the codebase. Existing saves load without issue.

g) Potential Improvements

  • Tech unlocks from anomalies — The techUnlock field exists on AnomalyReward but is not yet wired to grant actual technologies. This would allow rare anomalies to unlock unique techs not in the standard tree.
  • Investigation progress UI — Client-side progress bar showing investigation completion percentage (data is available via ticksCompleted / totalTicks).
  • Multi-chapter quest lines — Chain anomalies together (investigating one reveals the next) for species-specific storylines, as suggested in the ES2 research.
  • Hazardous anomalies — Some anomaly types (spatial_rift, gravity_anomaly) could damage or destroy ships during investigation, adding risk.
  • Anomaly respawn — Late-game anomaly spawning to keep exploration relevant throughout the game.
  • Fleet experience from exploration — Investigating anomalies could grant crew experience, tying into the 9-level experience system.
  • Visible investigation on galaxy map — An icon or animation showing a fleet actively investigating an anomaly.
  • Competitive investigation — Alert players when an opponent starts investigating an anomaly they've discovered.
