Balance Observations

The following observations are for morning review. No balance changes have been implemented.

  • AI Expansion: All empires remained on 1 planet across all playtests (1500–2500 ticks). The AI colonisation logic may not be triggering effectively — empires are not expanding to nearby systems despite having resources and available planets. High Priority
  • Ship Production: Most empires only have 1 ship after 1500+ ticks. Only Thyriaq Rogue Fleet (aggressive AI) managed to build 42 ships by tick 2500. The AI ship production threshold may be too high, or construction capacity is being fully consumed by buildings. High Priority
  • Combat Engagement: Zero combat encounters in 2500 ticks of the nightly playtest despite two aggressive AI players. Fleets may not be receiving movement orders to engage. This is likely a consequence of the single-planet issue — fleets have no targets if empires don't expand. High Priority
  • Conquest Victory Unreachable: With conquest requiring 75% of colonised planets and no AI expansion occurring, the conquest victory condition is effectively unreachable. The threshold itself is reasonable, but AI expansion must function first. Medium Priority
  • Research Rate Disparity: Research-focused species dramatically outperform others. Luminari reached 131/300 techs (Anti-Matter age) whilst Drakmari managed only 7 techs (Nano Atomic) despite 2500 ticks. The research trait multiplier may be too steep — a trait of 7 vs 3 should not produce a 19× difference. Consider a diminishing returns curve for research output. Medium Priority
  • Credit Accumulation: Credits accumulate very rapidly with no significant drain. Luminari accumulated ~4.9M credits at ~2,432/tick. Without meaningful upkeep costs or spending sinks, credits become meaningless after the early game. Consider increasing fleet upkeep or adding population maintenance costs. Low Priority
  • Population Growth Curves: Population growth rates vary enormously: Pyrenth reached 186,713 pop whilst Drakmari had only 39,833 despite similar reproduction traits (7 vs 7). Environmental preference matching and planet habitability may be causing large swings. The Thyriaq population showed -25.6/tick growth rate, suggesting a population decline — worth investigating whether this is intended (espionage/unrest mechanic?) or a bug. Medium Priority
  • Mineral Accumulation: Minerals accumulate but are not being spent. Pyrenth reached 50,427 minerals. If ship production and building construction consumed minerals properly, these stocks should be lower. This reinforces the AI-not-building-ships observation. Low Priority
