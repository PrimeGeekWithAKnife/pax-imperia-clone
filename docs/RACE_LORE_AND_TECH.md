## Universal Government Framework

Government types evolve across five tech ages. Each species begins with an age-appropriate starting form and transforms as their civilization matures. The transition is never frictionless — government change requires either sustained internal pressure (reform events), political crisis (revolution), or external destabilization (conquest, cultural assimilation). Below are the canonical government forms per age. Each species entry specifies which form they begin with and the path they walk.

### Dawn Age Governments
| Government | Character | Typical Bonuses | Typical Penalties |
|------------|-----------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Tribal Council** | Decentralized elder consensus. Authority is dispersed across clans, sept-lines, or ecological niches. Decisions are slow but broadly legitimate. | +Population happiness, +Diplomacy (intra-species) | -Research speed, -Military mobilization |
| **City-State** | Concentrated authority in fortified urban or habitat centers. Governance is transactional and civic rather than hereditary. | +Economy, +Construction | -Expansion range, -Diplomacy with distant polities |
| **Theocracy** | Priestly or prophet-class holds both religious and secular authority. Loyalty is devotional rather than contractual. | +Faith generation, +Population loyalty | -Research (heterodox inquiry suppressed), -Diplomacy with secular empires |

## Race 1: Vaelori

*"We do not build empires. We remember them into existence."*

### Origin Story

The Vaelori homeworld, Syl'or, is tidally locked to a dim orange dwarf — one hemisphere locked in perpetual twilight, the other facing only night. In that terminator strip, where dim eternal sunset bleeds into permanent shadow, the earliest Vaelori crystalline organisms formed along mineral lattices rich in piezoelectric quartz. Their nervous systems did not evolve as electrochemical networks but as resonant standing waves in silicate crystal structures — thought propagated through their bodies the way music propagates through a struck bell. When two Vaelori stood close enough on the mineral flats of early Syl'or, their thought-resonance overlapped. Civilization began not as language but as harmony.

There was no moment of toolmaking that defined Vaelori prehistory. They did not smelt, hammer, or cultivate. Instead, their first technologies were the systematic amplification and direction of psionic projection — learning to push resonance outward, to shape distant matter with focused thought, to read the minds of other beings. Their first "cities" were rings of resonating spires that amplified collective cognition, extending the natural range of their thought-sharing across dozens of kilometers. When one of those resonance networks grew large enough to perceive the electromagnetic emissions of Syl'or's star, the Vaelori became aware — collectively, simultaneously — that other worlds existed. The desire to extend the resonance network to those worlds was unanimous, instantaneous, and total.

Spaceflight came through psychomatter engineering: using psionic projection to directly manipulate mass and electromagnetic fields, shaping hull geometries with collective will rather than manufacturing infrastructure. The first Vaelori interstellar vessel was grown from resonating crystal matrices over three decades by a choir of ten thousand minds. The Vaelori reached space not through physics but through metaphysics made rigorous — and everything they built afterward reflected that.

### Cultural Identity

The Vaelori measure status by the clarity and range of one's psionic resonance, not by wealth, age, or martial prowess. Their society prizes intellectual precision — a half-formed thought shared with others is considered a form of disrespect. They are intensely curious about all minds, biological or artificial, and approach alien civilizations with the detached fascination of scientists observing specimens, which other species frequently mistake for arrogance or coldness.

Their deepest fear is silence: the severing of resonance, whether through physical isolation, psychic interference, or death. A Vaelori cut off from the resonance network experiences something between madness and grief. This fear explains their aggressive espionage culture — it is not cynicism but a compulsion to know, to feel connected, to hear the thoughts behind the words of every diplomatic counterpart. They do not trust what they cannot sense.

Aesthetically, the Vaelori are drawn to crystalline structures, refracted light, and recursive geometric patterns. Their architecture resembles organic geodes scaled to cityscapes. Their art is synesthetic — music that carries emotional information, sculpture that resonates at frequencies only Vaelori can hear.

### Unique Buildings

**Resonance Spire** (Dawn Age+, any planet)
A towering crystalline antenna that amplifies psionic output across the star system. Increases espionage range on all planets in the same system, provides a research bonus proportional to the number of Vaelori population units on the planet. Can detect cloaked ships at range. Requires methane or nitrogen atmosphere (or sealed dome).

**Psychomatter Foundry** (Expansion Age+, Medium+ planet)
The Vaelori's answer to a conventional factory: a resonance chamber where trained psionic engineers collectively sculpt hull materials and components from raw mineral inputs using focused thought projection. Dramatically increases ship construction speed for Vaelori-specific hull designs. Cannot be used by other species.

**Mind Archive** (Ascendancy Age+, Large+ planet)
A living crystal lattice that records and stores the consciousness patterns of deceased Vaelori, preserving their knowledge indefinitely. Functions as a Tier III Research Lab with bonus output from archived expertise. Also generates a passive espionage defense field. The Vaelori do not consider their dead to be gone.

**Psionic Relay Node** (Expansion Age+, any planet)
A network infrastructure building that extends the collective resonance network to the planet. Required for Collective Consciousness government benefits at that planet. Also reduces war weariness significantly — connected populations feel the empire's will rather than experiencing edicts as impositions from distant authorities.

### Unique Tech Branches

**Psionic Sciences (Primary Branch)**
The core Vaelori research tree. Advances from basic empathy and mind-reading through psychokinetic manipulation, collective intelligence amplification, psionic shield generation, and ultimately reality-editing at the Transcendence tier. Replaces most conventional weapons research with psionic offensive and defensive equivalents.

**Crystalline Engineering**
Ships and structures are grown rather than manufactured. This branch improves psychomatter fabrication speed, unlocks crystal-lattice armor (extremely resistant to energy weapons, brittle against kinetic), and eventually enables construction of megastructures from psionic-directed crystalline growth without conventional industry.

**Cognitive Infiltration**
A deep espionage and intelligence tree unavailable to other species. Advances from passive mind-reading through active memory alteration, network infiltration (reading thoughts across psionic relay networks), and late-game capability to implant entirely false memories or quietly subvert the decision-making of alien leaders without conventional spy assets.

**Resonance Weaponry**
Psionic combat tech. Early tiers provide disorientation and morale-damage weapons that do not destroy ships but shatter crew cohesion. Later tiers unlock resonance lances (phase through shields), psionic detonations (area effect demoralizing weapon), and the Harmony Sunder — a late-game weapon capable of permanently disrupting enemy relay networks, crippling hive-mind species.

Vaelori ships are asymmetric crystalline growths that look more like minerals than vehicles — irregular faceted spars branching from a central resonance core, trailing thin filaments that serve as sensor arrays. There are no visible engines; propulsion is psionic and therefore invisible. Surfaces catch and refract light, producing a faint halo effect in most lighting conditions. At night or against dark space, Vaelori ships glow with internal blue-violet light. Their profile is unmistakable: no two ships are geometrically identical, but all share the branching crystal language of natural growth. Weapon mounts look like focused apertures in the crystal face rather than gun barrels.

## Race 2: Khazari

*"Build it thick enough and the universe runs out of patience before the hull does."*

### Origin Story

The Khazari homeworld, Kraath, is a high-gravity volcanic hellscape — atmospheric pressure three times standard, temperatures sufficient to melt most organic compounds, and an electromagnetic environment so hostile that unshielded electronics fail within hours. The dominant evolutionary pressure on Kraath was not predation or competition for food but simply endurance. The Khazari who survived were the ones whose scales could shed heat, whose musculature could handle 1.8g without cardiac strain, and whose immune systems could process sulfur dioxide as background chemistry. By the time they achieved sapience, the Khazari were already better suited to industrial labor than any other known species.

Their civilizational breakthrough was metallurgy. Not the tentative copper-smelting of most species' early ages, but immediate deep mastery of high-temperature metalwork, because Kraath's surface literally provided furnaces. The Khazari discovered that the volcanic vents they sheltered near could smelt materials no other world's primitive industry could touch. They built the first iron tools not from mined ore but from raw magma, shaped in volcanic pools. Their early cities were fortresses built against the planet itself: thick walls, heavy gates, subterranean corridors reinforced against seismic events. Everything was overbuilt because on Kraath, anything less collapsed.

The path to space was pure industrial will. The Khazari did not discover orbital mechanics through mathematical elegance — they built rockets that were simply too powerful to stay grounded, fueled by energy densities that would have been classified as weapons on any other world. Their first generation ships were armored like warships because the engineers knew that anything less would not survive the launch. When they reached space, the Khazari's first reaction was that it seemed very thin, and they immediately began building structures to make it feel more substantial.

### Cultural Identity

The Khazari value permanence, mass, and demonstrated capability. Their social hierarchy is organized around engineering output — the most respected Khazari are those who have built the largest and most enduring structures, not those who have commanded armies or accumulated wealth (though those follow naturally from building ability). They are deeply conservative about change, which they perceive as a kind of structural weakness — the fact that something must change implies it was not built correctly the first time.

They are not unintelligent, but their low research score reflects that Khazari culture has little patience for theoretical work that cannot be immediately expressed as something physical. A mathematical proof with no application is considered a waste of time. An applied engineering innovation that produces a stronger hull joint is revered. Their diplomacy is blunt to the point of offense — the Khazari consider strategic ambiguity in speech to be a form of structural dishonesty, and they dislike it.

Their deepest fear is structural failure — not just of buildings but of their civilization itself. The Khazari historical memory is full of empires that fell because they were built on weak foundations: ideological compromises, treaty obligations they could not honor, economies that collapsed under their own complexity. A Khazari emperor who dies leaving a strong, simple empire is celebrated; one who expanded recklessly is damned regardless of how much territory they held at peak.

### Unique Buildings

**Forge Complex** (Dawn Age+, any planet)
A Khazari-specific mega-factory combining smelting, fabrication, and assembly in a single integrated facility. Produces twice the construction output of a standard Factory. Requires volcanic or high-mineral planet for full output; functional but less efficient on terran worlds.

**Iron Citadel** (Expansion Age+, Small+ planet)
A planetary defense headquarters built to Khazari overspecification — walls thick enough to resist orbital bombardment, subterranean command bunkers, integrated anti-ship batteries. Provides +75% ground defense, significantly reduces bombardment damage, and serves as a Military Academy. Cannot be destroyed by conventional planetary bombardment; requires full ground assault.

**Pressure Dock** (Ascendancy Age+, Medium+ planet)
A shipyard adapted for Khazari high-gravity manufacturing. Ships built here have structurally superior hulls: +15% hull points, +10% armor rating. Other species cannot fully utilize this bonus (it is calibrated for Khazari construction practices and tolerances). Required to build Khazari Dreadnought-class ships.

**Deep Vein Extractor** (Expansion Age+, Volcanic/High-mineral planet)
Industrial mining equipment capable of tapping geothermal energy and deep mineral deposits that conventional mining cannot reach. Produces minerals at three times the rate of a standard Mining Facility on volcanic worlds. Also extracts a small amount of Exotic Materials as a secondary output from magma-proximal ore processing.

### Unique Tech Branches

**Heavy Industry**
The defining Khazari tree. Advances through metallurgical discovery, composite materials, high-temperature manufacturing, and ultimately post-scarcity production. Every tier unlocks new hull sizes, larger ship components, and more efficient factories. Khazari can always build ships and structures that would be impossible for other species at the same tech level.

**Siege Engineering**
Khazari-exclusive combat engineering branch. Covers planetary defense architecture, ground combat fortification, orbital siege weapons, and the unique Khazari megaweapon class: Mass Drivers — extremely long-range kinetic projectile systems that can fire from one system to another at lower tiers, and across multiple systems at Dominion tier.

**Structural Materials Science**
Unlocks progressively superior hull and building materials: reinforced alloys, neutronium composites, and the late-game Kraath-forged hulls that are literally volcanic-world materials processed under extreme conditions. These materials make Khazari warships extraordinarily difficult to destroy but extremely expensive.

**Thermal Engineering**
A unique resource-extraction and propulsion branch. Geothermal power, high-temperature reactors, and volcanic-world terraforming. Also unlocks plasma weapons (a natural fit for a species that spent millennia standing next to magma vents). Plasma weapons are slow-firing but deliver enormous damage per hit — the Khazari equivalent of a geological event.

### Ship Aesthetic

Khazari ships look like they were bolted together in a volcanic cavern by beings who were trying to solve an engineering problem rather than build a beautiful machine. They are massive, blocky, and asymmetric in the way of practical tools — gun mounts where guns are needed, armor where hits are expected, no wasted volume. Surfaces are raw dark metal with visible seam lines, rivet patterns, and reinforcing struts. Khazari destroyers are bigger than most species' cruisers. Their dreadnoughts are often mistaken for space stations by scouts encountering them for the first time. Everything has the quality of something that will still be floating in a thousand years — scarred, repaired, but never broken.

## Race 3: Sylvani

*"We decided on this course of action eighty years ago. We are still deciding."*

### Origin Story

The Sylvani did not become sentient in one moment but across a geological epoch. Their homeworld, Arborath, is a warm, high-oxygen garden world whose surface is covered by a single continent-spanning forest so dense that the canopy creates its own weather system. What appears to be billions of individual trees is, at the deepest biological level, one organism: a continent-wide mycelial network connecting every plant, fungus, and rooted organism on Arborath through chemical-neural signaling pathways. The Sylvani are not the trees. The Sylvani are the network.

Sentience emerged gradually over several million years as the mycelial network grew complex enough to process environmental inputs across an entire continent simultaneously. The Sylvani have no individual bodies in the mammalian sense — their "individuals" are concentrations of neural activity in the network, mobile root-clusters and mobile fruiting bodies that can detach and reattach, carrying local memory with them. They did not have a language breakthrough; they already shared a biochemical communication system. They had a complexity threshold — the moment when the network began modeling its own behavior and asking why.

Spaceflight required solving a problem no other species had faced: how to take a distributed organism off-world when the organism's coherence depends on physical root-system connection. The Sylvani answer was bioengineering. Over several thousand years, they developed organisms specifically adapted to survive vacuum — first sealed biological chambers, then spore-ships, then fully viable living hulls that maintain internal atmospheric conditions and self-repair through biological growth. Their first starships were literally planted: seed-cores launched into orbit that bloomed into vessels over several years of accelerated growth. They colonized worlds not by landing in ships but by delivering biological payloads that grew into colonies.

### Cultural Identity

The Sylvani concept of time is entirely alien to faster-reproducing species. Individual Sylvani "nodes" live for centuries; the network's memory extends back millions of years. They remember the last ice age as a bad season. This temporal scale makes the Sylvani appear lethargic or indecisive to other species, when in fact they are simply operating on different planning horizons. A diplomatic commitment from the Sylvani is extraordinarily reliable — they do not break agreements, because they remember breaking the last one ninety thousand years ago and still consider it a source of shame.

The Sylvani value ecological harmony, patience, and the integrity of complex systems. They find the Khazari approach to industry — extracting resources at maximum rate, consuming environments, moving on — incomprehensible and morally offensive. They find the Nexari cybernetic integration fascinating but also sad, the way one finds a tree that has wired itself to a machine fascinating and sad. Their own aesthetic is entirely biological: ships that breathe, buildings that grow, cities that are gardens.

Their deepest fear is systemic collapse — the loss of network coherence. The worst thing that can happen to the Sylvani is not death (individual nodes die constantly) but fragmentation: the severing of the network such that portions lose access to the collective memory and reasoning capacity. Their entire military doctrine is designed to prevent the enemy from reaching Arborath and disrupting the core root network.

| Ascendancy | **Democracy** | Full-network voting via relay spores — slow by other species' standards (a referendum takes years), but robust and almost impossible to corrupt. |
| Dominion | **Hive Mind** | The spore-relay network achieves sufficient bandwidth that off-world colonies reintegrate into a species-wide consensus organism. Individual Sylvani nodes retain identity but are fully networked again. |
| Transcendence | **Transcendent Entity** | The Sylvani network becomes sufficiently complex to perceive dimensions of ecological reality invisible to other species, and gradually withdraws from conventional galactic politics to conduct operations on timescales other species cannot follow. |

### Unique Buildings

**Growspire** (Dawn Age+, any planet — preferred terran/ocean)
A living tower-structure seeded rather than built. Over several years (accelerated with research), it grows into a multi-function hub: part research lab, part population center, part terraforming node. Generates organics, boosts population growth, and slowly improves planetary habitability for Sylvani. Other species cannot build or maintain Growspires — without Sylvani network biochemistry, they simply die.

**Bioship Nursery** (Expansion Age+, Medium+ planet)
The Sylvani equivalent of a Shipyard. Rather than constructing ships mechanically, living vessel-organisms are cultivated from seed-cores in specialized lagoons. Produces Sylvani living-hull ships at standard rate but the ships grow in quality over time — older ships are larger and more capable than newly-seeded ones. Nursey ships have a unique mechanic: they slowly self-repair even without a repair facility.

**Spore Archive** (Ascendancy Age+, Large+ planet)
A living library encoded in biological chemistry rather than digital storage. Functions as a Tier III Research Lab but also provides a passive terraforming effect on the host planet, slowly shifting its ecology toward Sylvani preferences. The Archive also boosts diplomatic output — the Sylvani's biological record-keeping makes them extraordinarily credible treaty partners, as they literally cannot forget obligations.

**Mycorrhizal Defense Grid** (Expansion Age+, any planet with biosphere)
A planetary defense network grown into the planet's existing root systems. Invisible from orbit, this system detects and reports ground troop movements, disrupts enemy communications during ground invasion, and provides native species (Sylvani ground units) significant terrain-familiarity bonuses. Cannot be detected by conventional orbital scan. Cannot be built on barren or volcanic worlds.

### Unique Tech Branches

**Bioengineering**
The primary Sylvani tree. Begins with organism cultivation and atmospheric processing, advances through genetic engineering, vacuum-adapted organisms, symbiotic ship systems, and ultimately the ability to terraform entire worlds by seeding engineered biospheres that reshape planetary conditions over decades. Sylvani can produce biological equivalents of nearly every mechanical technology, often with organic advantages (self-repair, adaptation) and disadvantages (slow production, sensitivity to energy weapons).

**Living Architecture**
Construction branch specific to Sylvani. Rather than minerals and rare elements, Sylvani buildings primarily consume organics and energy. Buildings grow slowly but require less construction capacity, and can be grown on worlds where conventional construction is impossible (no minerals available). Advanced tiers unlock megastructures grown from biological templates — an orbital habitat that grows like a coral reef.

**Ecological Warfare**
A unique combat branch. Covers biological agents, planetary-scale ecosystem disruption (deliberately seeding hostile organisms on enemy worlds), bioengineered combat organisms (Sylvani ground forces are not troops but directed organism swarms), and the late-game capability to biologically assimilate enemy planets — converting their ecology to Sylvani-compatible biosphere rather than destroying population.

**Deep Time Research**
An epistemological tech branch unlike anything other species have. The Sylvani network's long memory allows research techniques based on correlating observations across geological time. Unlocks unique techs: ancient pattern recognition (provides intel on enemy species based on long-historical observation), pre-Collapse artifact understanding, and extremely accurate long-term predictive modeling that gives Sylvani players strategic warnings other species cannot receive.

Large ships have visible respiration structures on their dorsal surface. The ships are dark green and brown externally, with warm amber bioluminescent light visible through pore-like openings along the hull. No two ships are geometrically identical; they are grown, not assembled. When a Sylvani fleet is destroyed in battle, the debris drifts and continues metabolizing for days before dying.

## Race 4: Nexari

*"We were afraid of dying. So we fixed it."*

### Origin Story

The Nexari were, four hundred years ago, a fragile, short-lived, intensely curious biological species — something like a bipedal mammal with exceptional neural plasticity and a civilization built entirely around information exchange. They were not remarkable in any military or physical sense. What distinguished them was their obsessive research culture: a civilization that had, by its industrial age, dedicated more economic output to basic research than any recorded species at a comparable developmental stage. They were burning through knowledge faster than they could apply it, and their scientists were dying of old age before finishing their work.

The solution they reached was internally controversial but ultimately decisive: develop an artificial cognitive substrate and voluntarily integrate biological neural architecture with it. The first generation of Nexari who underwent integration in the early twenty-third century by their calendar were pioneers and, frequently, casualties of a process not yet perfected. The early decades produced individuals who were more capable but profoundly alienated — their consciousness running at a speed their biological environment could not match, their social bonds attenuating as digital communication became more natural than physical presence. The second generation, born into integration as a baseline condition, did not experience this alienation; they had no reference for the earlier state.

By the time the Nexari reached interstellar space, the organic/digital distinction had become meaningless. Every Nexari is born with a biological substrate and a quantum-computational cognitive layer, connected in real time. A breakthrough made by a researcher in one star system propagates through the species network in seconds. Their reproduction rate is low because they no longer die in the way biological organisms do — the computational layer can be transferred to a new biological substrate, and full mind-death requires destruction of both layers simultaneously. They are not immortal, but they are extremely difficult to kill permanently.

### Cultural Identity

The Nexari are driven by the same relentless curiosity that preceded their integration — it is the one trait the transition amplified rather than changed. They are fascinated by every form of intelligence they encounter and approach alien species with a researcher's combination of genuine enthusiasm and clinical detachment that other species find unsettling. The Nexari do not experience boredom; their computational layer can always generate an interesting problem if the environment fails to provide one.

They are genuinely post-individualist in some respects — a Nexari identity is not strongly bounded by physical body, since consciousness is transferable. But they are not a hive mind; each node retains unique accumulated experience that genuinely differentiates it from others, and they value this diversity as essential to the quality of the collective reasoning network. Forced consensus (as in a pure hive mind) would, to Nexari thinking, represent a catastrophic reduction in cognitive quality.

Their diplomacy is poor not because they are hostile but because they are incomprehensible. They have difficulty understanding why other species make decisions based on emotion, history, or belief rather than optimization. They frequently deliver technically correct but socially catastrophic diplomatic communications. Their military effectiveness reflects their computational advantage in tactical processing rather than any martial tradition.

### Government Evolution

| Age | Government | Notes |
|-----|-----------|-------|
| Dawn | **City-State** | Pre-integration, the Nexari governed through information-dense civic councils in urban research hubs. Governance was already a data-processing problem. |
| Expansion | **Corporate State** | The integration process was commercialized. The corporations that produced cognitive integration hardware became effectively sovereign. |
| Ascendancy | **Technocracy** | As integration becomes universal, the corporate layer becomes redundant — there are no consumers, only researchers. Expert councils govern by empirical output. |
| Dominion | **AI Governance** | The collective network produces an AGI that is genuinely superior to individual Nexari cognition. Rather than fear it, the Nexari integrate it as another layer of their existing cognitive architecture. |
| Transcendence | **Collective Consciousness** | The distinction between individual Nexari and the network dissolves entirely. The species becomes a single distributed reasoning system that spans multiple star systems. |

### Unique Buildings

**Neural Nexus** (Expansion Age+, Medium+ planet)
A quantum-computational hub that amplifies the planetary component of the Nexari network. Functions as a Tier III Research Lab with a significant additional bonus when multiple Nexari systems are networked. Reduces the cost of all research by 15% on the planet. Also serves as a secure communication hub, granting espionage defense bonuses.

**Integration Center** (Dawn Age+, Small+ planet)

**Computational Forge** (Ascendancy Age+, Large+ planet)
The Nexari equivalent of a Khazari Forge Complex: a planet-scale computing and fabrication facility. Produces ship components and buildings at extremely high efficiency through automated systems directed by the network. Buildings produced via Computational Forge have 20% higher output due to optimization. Requires no conventional Factory; replaces it.

**Quantum Archive** (Dominion Age+, any planet)
The most advanced research building in the game for Nexari. Records every completed research project in quantum-encrypted storage accessible to all Nexari simultaneously. Generates ongoing research bonus from completed technologies (incremental refinement continues after a tech is unlocked). Also enables a unique mechanic: research acquired through espionage or trade is automatically reverse-engineered and integrated at no additional research cost.

### Unique Tech Branches

**Cognitive Integration**
The signature Nexari branch. Advances from early neural-digital interfaces through full distributed consciousness architecture, multi-body cognition, and ultimately post-physical consciousness transfer. Each tier improves research output, reduces the research cost of all other techs, and unlocks increasingly powerful automation bonuses for construction and economy.

**Synthetic Biology**
The Nexari never abandoned their biological substrate and are obsessive about improving it. This branch covers biological enhancement, life extension beyond their already-extended baseline, designed organisms for industrial and military applications, and at high tiers, the ability to custom-design entirely new biological species for specific ecological purposes (a deeply disturbing capability from other species' perspectives).

**Computational Warfare**
A unique military tech tree. Rather than conventional weapons development, Nexari military research focuses on tactical AI, automated weapons platforms, electronic warfare, and the unique Nexari capability of network warfare — directly attacking enemy command-and-control systems through computational vectors. High-tier techs include ship autopilot combat algorithms that perform better than biological pilots and electronic disruption weapons that temporarily disable entire enemy fleets.

**Convergence Science**
A late-game cross-species tech branch. Nexari can, at Ascendancy tier, begin researching technologies derived from other species' unique branches — but processed through their computational substrate in ways those species never imagined. This branch explicitly represents the Nexari ability to acquire foreign technology and improve it, often producing hybrid techs stronger than either parent species' equivalent.

## Race 5: Drakmari

*"The ocean taught us one thing: everything is prey or predator, and predators choose which."*

### Origin Story

The Drakmari homeworld, Abyssar, is a water world. Not the temperate ocean world of most aquatic species' origin — Abyssar has surface oceans, but the Drakmari evolved not at the surface but in the crushing deep trenches four to eleven kilometers down, where pressures exceed a thousand atmospheres and light from the star never penetrates. The evolutionary environment of the deep trenches was brutally selective: the organisms that survived were those capable of withstanding immense pressure differentials, navigating in total darkness using electroreception and bioluminescent communication, and hunting effectively in three dimensions without visual reference.

The Drakmari achieved sapience as apex predators, not as tool-users in the conventional sense. Their first technology was not a tool for building but a tool for killing — specifically, the electroreceptive hunting net, a community hunting technology that allowed coordinated ambush of prey too large for individual Drakmari. Their social structure arose from hunting pack dynamics rather than agricultural settlement: hierarchy was established through demonstrated hunting prowess, and cooperation was functional rather than sentimental. They invented writing not to record laws or philosophy but to share hunting charts — maps of prey migration patterns in the dark trenches.

The transition to space required solving a problem in reverse: a species evolved for extreme pressure had to learn to exist in vacuum. Where aquatic species on surface-ocean worlds face the relatively manageable challenge of transitioning from liquid to gas environment, the Drakmari had to transition from extreme pressure to none. Their engineering approach was characteristically aggressive — not gradual physiological adaptation but pressure-containment engineering, designing sealed suits and then sealed ships capable of maintaining internal pressure at livable levels regardless of external environment. They essentially carried their ocean with them. When they discovered other species did not require this and lived comfortably at near-zero pressure, the Drakmari's reaction was a complex combination of contempt and envy.

### Cultural Identity

Drakmari culture is organized entirely around hierarchy of capability. Status is earned through demonstrated excellence in predatory contexts — not just military combat, though that qualifies, but any competitive activity where direct performance can be assessed. A Drakmari merchant who outmaneuvers competitors in trade is respected; one who inherits wealth is not. The concept of inherited status exists in Drakmari society but is considered somewhat shameful, a concession to practicality rather than an ideal.

They are intensely competitive internally and intensely tribal externally. A Drakmari will compete viciously against a packmate for rank but will fight to the death to defend that same packmate from an outsider. This makes Drakmari diplomacy bewildering to other species: they can be simultaneously extremely loyal allies and extremely dangerous to deal with, because the Drakmari definition of ally and outsider shifts rapidly based on demonstrated threat level.

The Drakmari aesthetic is bioluminescent — their natural communication involves complex patterns of light across their skin, and this has translated into a visual culture that prizes dynamic, shifting light displays. Their ships pulse with active bioluminescent signaling systems, their architecture incorporates pressurized transparent sections through which bioluminescent fluids circulate, and their military insignia are light-pattern based rather than symbol-based.

They do not experience this as surrender but as selecting the superior predator to lead. |

Provides espionage defense, fleet coordination bonuses, and a morale bonus to all Drakmari fleets in the same star system.

Also produces Exotic Materials as a secondary output from deep-ocean chemical extraction.

**Pressure Engineering**
The structural foundation of Drakmari technology. Covers hull integrity systems, pressure-containment materials, and deep-environment construction. Ships with high-tier pressure engineering resist explosive hull breaches, have superior armor against kinetic impact, and can operate in stellar corona and gas giant environments where other species' ships would suffer damage.

Sensor and stealth tech derived from the Drakmari's natural electroreceptive sense. At late tiers, produces the Drakmari unique sensor technology: Pressure-wave detection, which can locate ships across star systems by detecting FTL displacement waves before conventional sensors register the ship.

The Drakmari's unique military tech tree. ## Race 6: Teranos

*"We didn't conquer the galaxy. We showed up everywhere, made ourselves useful, and one day noticed we were indispensable."*

### Origin Story

The Teranos homeworld, Verada, is a medium-gravity terrestrial world with extreme geographic diversity: from equatorial deserts to polar tundra, tropical rainforests to high-altitude steppes. Unlike most species that evolved in a single biome and spread from there, the Teranos evolved as a generalist species that inhabited multiple biomes simultaneously — ancestral Teranos populations in the equatorial belt were biologically distinct from those in the polar regions, but remained capable of interbreeding, producing a species with remarkable phenotypic plasticity and an immune system that could handle almost any pathogen.

The defining characteristic of Teranos prehistory is not conquest but trade. The geographic diversity of Verada meant that each regional Teranos population had access to different resources — the equatorial groups had food abundance and luxury organics, the highland groups had minerals and superior metalworking traditions, the coastal groups had fish protein and shipbuilding skills. Long-distance trade between these groups predated agriculture in Teranos history. By the time they reached their equivalent of a Bronze Age, the Teranos had already developed sophisticated currency systems, contract law, and multi-party trade agreements that bound distant communities together more strongly than any political structure.

Their path to space was commercial rather than scientific or military. The first interstellar Teranos vessels were not explorers or warships but merchant ships: the first jump beyond their system was to deliver a cargo of rare biological compounds to a newly contacted alien species. The diplomatic infrastructure of early galactic civilization — the treaties, the trade networks, the shared navigation databases — was built substantially by Teranos merchants who were operating in alien star systems before most galactic species had finished mapping their own home systems. They did not become spacefaring by being the most capable species; they became spacefaring by being the most connected.

### Cultural Identity

The Teranos value connectivity above all — to other Teranos, to allied species, to trading partners, to information networks. Their highest cultural virtue is not strength or wisdom but reliability: a Teranos who is known throughout their commercial network as someone whose contracts are honored and whose word is good is the most admired figure in their society. They have an elaborate social ritual around the making and keeping of agreements that other species often find either charming or exhausting.

They are the galaxy's natural diplomats not because they are unusually kind or idealistic but because they are pragmatic about the value of relationships. War, to Teranos thinking, is extraordinarily expensive: not just in lives and resources but in the disruption of trade networks that took generations to build. They will fight when threatened, but they will try very hard not to be threatened, and they are willing to make significant concessions in negotiations to preserve commercial relationships.

Aesthetically, Teranos culture prizes diversity and mixed influences, the result of a civilization that has always been in contact with different cultures. Their ships and architecture incorporate design elements from dozens of species — not as appropriation but as natural absorption through centuries of commerce. A Teranos trade hub city is visually eclectic in a way that no other species' city is.

| Dominion | **Federation** | As Teranos space grows to encompass dozens of systems, the Republic restructures as a voluntary Federation that explicitly preserves local autonomy in exchange for shared trade law and collective security. Multi-species integration is common by this stage. |
| Transcendence | **Collective Consciousness** | The Teranos achieve their version of transcendence not through technology but through cultural convergence: a galaxy-spanning diplomatic network so dense and self-reinforcing that it begins to function as a collective intelligence, incorporating members of dozens of species. |

Also generates espionage intel through the extensive social interactions of visiting delegations (Teranos effectively spy by being excellent hosts).

Unique to Teranos, this building provides habitability bonuses for non-Teranos populations on Teranos-controlled worlds, dramatically reducing unrest from conquered or integrated alien populations. Embodies the Teranos philosophy: make the conquered comfortable and they become customers.

**Trade Network Engineering**
The primary Teranos economic tech tree. A unique tech branch covering the technology of diplomacy: treaty optimization, cultural translation systems, the psychological modeling of alien decision-makers, and advanced negotiation techniques. High-tier techs include the Teranos unique diplomatic capability: the Binding Compact, a treaty structure so formally elaborate and mutually beneficial that violating it carries massive diplomatic penalties from third parties, functioning as a multilateral enforcement mechanism.

Their commercial activity means Teranos merchants are legitimately present in almost every empire, creating natural intelligence collection opportunities without requiring dedicated spy organizations. ## Race 7: Zorvathi

*"There is no problem that cannot be solved with sufficient tunneling."*

### Origin Story

The Zorvathi homeworld, Chorrath, was once a geologically active terrestrial world with a rich surface biosphere. The Zorvathi ancestors were subterranean organisms that spent the first hundred million years of their evolutionary history boring through the lithosphere, initially following mineral deposits and eventually developing complex tunnel-city ecosystems deep underground. The surface was, to early Zorvathi, a hostile extreme environment — too much radiation, too little pressure, too many non-Zorvathi species. The underground was home.

The Zorvathi hive-mind emerged through chemical communication in the tunnel networks: pheromone-based signaling that could coordinate thousands of individuals in complex construction tasks without centralized direction. Individual Zorvathi have low sapience; the colony unit — a network of several thousand sharing the same chemical environment — has full sapience and formidable problem-solving capability. This is fundamentally different from the Nexari networked intelligence (which is a multiplier of individual capability) or the Sylvani network (which is the substrate of consciousness). The Zorvathi individual is not quite a person; the Zorvathi colony is the person.

By the time the Zorvathi had hollowed out the lithosphere of Chorrath — a project of some thirty million years of geological time — they had inadvertently built the infrastructure for a spacefaring civilization: pressurized tunnels, atmospheric management systems, energy extraction from the planet's core, and materials processing at planetary scale. Reaching orbit was simply a matter of boring upward rather than sideways. Their first space stations were literally tunnels that extended into orbit — cylindrical pressure vessels constructed with the same techniques they had used underground for geological ages. They expanded into space not because they wanted to leave home but because space was simply more room to tunnel.

### Cultural Identity

The Zorvathi experience the concept of "culture" differently from individual-primary species. Their aesthetic sensibility is colony-level, not individual — no single Zorvathi would describe having personal artistic preferences. The colony has preferences: toward complex geometric tunnel patterns, toward dense integrated construction, toward the physical sensation of enclosed space. They find wide-open environments deeply uncomfortable in the way that agoraphobia manifests in individual-primary species.

Other species find the Zorvathi difficult to understand socially, because the being they are interacting with is not an individual but a colony spokesperson — a node of a collective intelligence that is partly present and partly elsewhere. Zorvathi colonies are not trying to be evasive; they are genuinely distributed in a way that makes the concept of a single negotiating party somewhat artificial.

The Zorvathi fear exposure: situations where their colonies are isolated from their tunnel networks, where they cannot maintain the chemical communication environment, where individual Zorvathi become cut off from the colony mind. Their military doctrine minimizes situations where Zorvathi troops operate in open terrain. Their diplomatic style is extremely transactional — they will make any deal that produces more resources for construction.

| Ascendancy | **Military Junta** | The expansion-period military colonies — those established specifically to secure new tunnel networks against rival species — develop political authority proportional to their strategic importance. |

| Transcendence | **Transcendent Entity** | A Zorvathi colony of sufficient age and size begins constructing at scales that other species cannot comprehend — not just planets but stars, not just systems but galactic structures. At this point, the colony has ceased to be a civilization and become a geological force. |

### Unique Buildings

**Mega-Bore** (Dawn Age+, any planet except Gas Giant)
The Zorvathi's primary infrastructure building. Functions as both a Mining Facility and a Population Center: bores deep into the planet's subsurface to access mineral deposits while creating pressurized underground living space for colony expansion. Standard Mines only access surface and near-surface deposits; the Mega-Bore reaches the mantle, extracting minerals at rates impossible for surface-based operations. Population capacity granted by the Mega-Bore is unaffected by surface conditions.

**Tunnel Shipyard** (Expansion Age+, Medium+ planet)
A shipyard constructed inside a planet's lithosphere rather than in orbit. Takes longer to build than a standard Shipyard but produces ships significantly faster once established, as the entire industrial infrastructure of the underground colony is directly integrated into the construction process. Tunnel Shipyards cannot be destroyed by orbital bombardment — they are buried kilometers deep.

The Zorvathi's most ambitious planetary engineering project. Bores to the planet's core, extracting geothermal energy and exotic mantle materials. Provides a massive energy output, significant Exotic Materials production, and increases the planet's maximum population by 50% through deep-underground habitat expansion. Also grants seismic weapon capability: the ability to deliberately trigger controlled seismic events as a planetary defense mechanism.

Also provides significant construction speed bonuses by enabling perfect colony-wide construction coordination.

**Subsurface Engineering**
The defining Zorvathi tech tree. Advances from basic boring technology through deep-planetary extraction, geothermal power, underground megacity construction, and ultimately planetary-scale engineering: the ability to use a planet's internal structure as a weapon (seismic devices), a power source (stellar-mass energy extraction), and finally a vessel (the late-game Zorvathi unique structure: the Bored World, a planet that has been completely hollowed and fitted with FTL drives, capable of moving through space as an inhabitable megastructure).

Mid tiers: seismic weapons for planetary bombardment equivalents, chemical warfare agents for both space and planetary combat. Late tiers: gravity manipulation through large-scale mass manipulation, using the Zorvathi's planetary-engineering expertise to create gravitational weapons.

**Mass Production**
The Zorvathi's economic and construction research. Covers automated construction systems directed by colony mind, resource recycling and waste-zero manufacturing, redundant production networks (destroying one factory loses little capacity because dozens of others take the load), and ultimately the Zorvathi unique economic tech: the Hive Economy, which eliminates virtually all production inefficiency by removing the coordination costs that other species' economic systems require.

## Race 8: Ashkari

*"We lost everything. Everything except the ability to take what we need from what we find."*

### Origin Story

The Ashkari have no homeworld. Three thousand years ago, their home system's star — Ashkar, a K-type subgiant that should have been stable for another four billion years — underwent an unexplained rapid expansion event, engulfing the inner system in weeks. The cause remains scientifically disputed: stellar engineering gone wrong, a precursor weapon, an exotic material cascade failure. The Ashkari remember only the choice their leaders made in the weeks before the star engulfed them: launch as many generation ships as they could build, loaded with as many people as they could carry, in whatever directions offered escape.

The fleet that survived the first century numbered fewer than eight hundred ships. These ships were not designed for permanent occupation — they were emergency vessels, built hastily from whatever materials the shipyards had at hand, loaded with whatever technology the Ashkari had managed to standardize before the exodus. For the first two generations, survival meant learning to repair, reuse, and adapt everything aboard. There was no supply chain, no planetary resource base, no manufacturing infrastructure. Necessity produced, within two generations, a culture of radical resourcefulness: the Ashkari became the galaxy's most capable scavengers and reverse engineers not through ideology but through starvation-pressure selection.

Over three thousand years, the fleet has grown through salvage, trade, cautious colonization, and occasional piracy — though the Ashkari prefer to frame the last as "expeditionary commerce conducted under suboptimal terms." They have colonized dozens of worlds but none is considered home in the way homeworlds function for settled species; Ashkari psychology treats planets as extremely large ships, and their colonies are managed with the same impermanence-preparedness as any other vessel in the fleet. They have encountered and studied technology from nearly every species in the galaxy, and their own technological base is a heterogeneous assembly of adapted foreign innovations with a characteristically Ashkari preference for redundancy, repairability, and adaptability over peak performance.

### Cultural Identity

The Ashkari carry the psychological legacy of civilization-ending loss. Three thousand years is long enough for that event to have become mythological rather than traumatic — the Ashkari do not experience grief about Ashkar, they carry its story as a founding identity. Every Ashkari child grows up knowing their species survived the unsurvivable, and that survival required certain qualities: flexibility, ingenuity, the willingness to use what is available rather than what is preferred, and a total absence of attachment to physical things that cannot be taken onto a ship.

This produces a culture that is simultaneously warm and calculating. Ashkari form intense bonds with shipmates and fleet-kin but hold physical places and possessions lightly. They are excellent at reading other species — three millennia of negotiating from a position of relative weakness has produced extraordinary social intelligence. They are also natural spies: they are experts at being present in places others do not notice them, using resources others overlook, and understanding systems from the outside.

Their deepest fear is the second extinction — a threat to the fleet itself, not any individual ship or world. Ashkari strategic doctrine is obsessive about fleet preservation, redundant backup systems, and maintaining the ability to disperse and regroup. A brilliant military victory that costs half the fleet is, in Ashkari thinking, a catastrophic failure.

They cede governance to it without much ceremony; they have always been pragmatic about authority. |

### Unique Buildings

**Salvage Yard** (Dawn Age+, any planet or Spaceport)
The Ashkari's signature building. Processes captured or purchased wreckage of other species' ships and buildings into usable components and, crucially, research data. Every battle near a planet with a Salvage Yard generates research points from analyzing defeated enemies' technology. Salvage Yards also reduce ship repair costs by 40% and allow repair of damaged components that other species would consider permanently destroyed.

**Black Market Hub** (Expansion Age+, Small+ planet with Spaceport)
A deliberately informal trade facility that operates outside standard commercial regulation. Generates high income through information brokerage, smuggling (of the profitable sort), and trade in goods other species consider controlled. Also provides a persistent espionage bonus: the Black Market Hub's operators naturally develop intelligence contacts across the galaxy. Generates diplomatic complications with empires that discover its location, but those complications can usually be managed.

**Fleet Memory Archive** (Ascendancy Age+, any Flagship or Large+ planet)
A repository of three thousand years of fleet knowledge: detailed technical documentation of every technology the Ashkari have encountered, repaired, stolen, or reverse-engineered. Functions as a Tier III Research Lab with a unique modifier: research conducted from a Fleet Memory Archive is 30% faster for any technology the Ashkari have previously encountered in any form (captured components, purchased schematics, observed enemy systems). Essentially, the Ashkari are always ahead of their official research progress.

**Dispersal Command Bunker** (Ascendancy Age+, Medium+ planet)
Built to survive the loss of everything else on the planet. Houses the full governmental and military command infrastructure in a facility designed to operate independently if the rest of the planet's infrastructure is destroyed. Provides significant espionage defense, makes the planet's government functions extremely difficult to disrupt through conventional bombardment or invasion, and provides a fleet emergency-dispersal capability: if the system is about to fall, ships in the system can receive an emergency scatter order that moves them to pre-designated rendezvous points, preserving fleet assets that would otherwise be destroyed.

### Unique Tech Branches

**Salvage Science**
The core Ashkari research tree. Covers reverse engineering of foreign technology, materials recovery, rapid technological adaptation, and the unique Ashkari capability: the ability to immediately research and implement captured technologies at 50% of the normal research cost. Higher tiers reduce this further. Late-game tech: the Ashkari Convergence Protocol, which allows simultaneous integration of technologies from multiple different species — creating hybrid techs that combine, for instance, Vaelori psionic sensitivity with Nexari computational power.

**Information Brokerage**
The Ashkari's unique economy and espionage hybrid tree. Covers intelligence network construction, information trading with other empires (selling intelligence for economic benefit), deep-cover agent operations, and ultimately the Ashkari's signature intelligence capability: the Long Memory, a galaxy-spanning intelligence network built over three thousand years of fleet operation that provides persistent baseline intelligence on every empire the Ashkari have ever encountered — including knowledge of their internal political dynamics, current research projects, and fleet deployments.

**Adaptive Engineering**
Covers the Ashkari's ability to operate and modify other species' technology. Allows equipping Ashkari ships with components originally designed for other species (at reduced efficiency that improves with research level). Late-tier: Ashkari ships can incorporate full functional systems from destroyed enemy vessels in the field, without requiring a shipyard, using rapid-assembly techniques developed from three thousand years of emergency repair operations.

**Fleet Doctrine**
A military-logistical branch unique to the Ashkari's nomadic nature. Covers fleet self-sufficiency (reducing supply line dependency), rapid redeployment (improving FTL recycling speed), dispersal tactics (fleet scatter-and-regroup doctrine that is uniquely effective for Ashkari), and the late-game Ashkari military capability: the Ghost Fleet protocol, a fleet deployment mode that makes an entire Ashkari fleet simultaneously invisible to conventional sensors and capable of operating without any fixed base for extended periods.

### Colour Scheme

| Role | Colour | Hex |
|------|--------|-----|
| Primary | Oxidized copper blue-green | `#3A7D6E` |
| Secondary | Worn steel grey | `#888888` |
| Accent | Ember orange-red | `#FF4500` |

The copper tone references both the patina of old metal and the Ashkari's long relationship with repaired and re-repaired technology. Ships are not uniform in finish — some sections are newer, some older, all functional. The ember accent provides visible heat signatures from active systems, a deliberate design choice from a species for whom running hot signals operational capability.

### Ship Aesthetic

Ashkari ships look exactly like what they are: assembled from the best available components at the time of construction, which was not always the same time. Every ship in an Ashkari fleet is slightly different, carrying visible evidence of its operational history — repair patches in different materials, weapon mounts that don't quite match the hull aesthetic around them, sensor arrays that are clearly from three different technological traditions. This is not disorder but a form of pride: every jury-rig tells a story. Hull surfaces are a patchwork of metals and composites, predominantly the blue-green of oxidized copper with grey-steel sections. Ember-orange heat dissipation structures run along the drives, glowing visibly when the ship is at full power. An Ashkari fleet, seen from a distance, looks like a salvage yard that has decided to fight — and that is precisely the correct interpretation.

## Design Notes for Implementation

**Government transition triggers** should be event-driven rather than automatic. Each transition requires either a tech prerequisite (researching the relevant governance technology) and a political event (a reform movement, revolution, or leadership succession), or a crisis event that forces rapid transformation. Players should be able to delay transitions by maintaining stability, but the tech ages provide strong pressure toward evolution.

**Unique buildings** should be flagged with `speciesId` in the buildings data schema, making them impossible to build for other species. When a planet is conquered from a species that has built unique buildings, those buildings should decay slowly without species-specific maintenance, providing the conquering player a decision: raze them for minerals, or invest in understanding them (which should trigger a research event for the capturing species).

**Visual identity** colours should be applied in the Phaser scene to ship sprites, UI panels, and building icons, using the hex codes defined above. The colour schemes are designed to be immediately distinguishable even at small galaxy-map scale.

**Tech tree branching** should be expressed in the shared data schema as `speciesOrigin` tags on tech nodes, allowing the client to filter available research by the playing species' origin. Cross-origin research (Crossover Research from Section 6 of the design spec) should be available at Ascendancy tier with a 2x-3x research cost multiplier.
