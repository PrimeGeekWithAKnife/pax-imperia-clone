# Colony Vision — Director's Notes

## Population Model — Full Demographics

Population is not a single number. It's a living society with segments:

### Age Demographics
- **Young** — Need shelter, food, and education. Do NOT contribute to the workforce. A cost centre, but the future.
- **Working age** — The productive core. But not all choose the same path (see vocations below).
- **Elderly** — Can contribute financially and to research, but cannot fight. Wisdom and wealth, not muscle.

### Vocations (within working-age population)
- Only a **portion** of the population WANTS to be fighters, scientists, workers, etc.
- Building labs without scientists is pointless — you need educated people to fill them
- Education infrastructure determines how many people CAN become specialists
- **You can build all the labs in the world, but without scientists and educated people to become scientists, you just won't benefit**
- Vocational distribution is influenced by: education buildings, government type, species traits, happiness, cultural values

### Happiness & Unrest
Unhappy populations have consequences:
- **Protests** — Production slowdowns, public disorder
- **Refusal to procreate** — Population growth stalls or reverses
- **Building destruction** — Angry mobs damage infrastructure
- **Revolt** — Open rebellion, potential civil war
- **Defection** — Population segments flee to neighbouring empires

## Multi-Species Colonies

After unification, conquest, or integration:

- Multiple species coexist on the same planet with **combined stats**
- The indigenous/conquered population may be:
  - **Inherently unhappy** after hostile takeover
  - **Prone to violence and civil war**
  - **Slow to settle** — takes a long time without intervention
- Special measures needed to manage multi-species worlds:
  - **Incentives** — Better housing, cultural concessions, self-governance rights
  - **Appeasement** — Religious freedom, language preservation, cultural districts
  - **Restraint** — Martial law, curfews, surveillance (effective but breeds resentment)
- **Governor demands** — Populations may demand a brutal governor be removed in favour of a nicer one. Ignoring this demand escalates unrest.
- A Khazari factory on a Sylvani world? Ecological protests. Cultural friction is real and species-specific.

## Planetary Governors

Governors are **characters**, not just modifier bonuses:

### Personality & Traits
- Each governor has a distinct personality affecting ALL aspects of the planet:
  - Population happiness and growth
  - Economic output (credits, trade)
  - Energy and mineral production
  - Research output
  - Military readiness and recruitment
  - Building construction speed
- Some governors are brilliant administrators, others are corrupt, some are cruel but effective

### Governor Agency
- **Corrupt governors** may skim resources — you get less than you should
- **Brilliant governors** can boost production beyond base numbers
- **Disloyal governors** may defect to an enemy empire, taking the planet with them
- **Brutal governors** keep order efficiently but breed deep resentment
- **Popular governors** are loved by the people but may develop their own ambitions
- Governors should be appointable, removable, and sometimes assassinated (espionage)

## Minor Species — The Trojan Horse

### Detection
- **If the player has a spy network and population surveillance**, they have a CHANCE to uncover a plot
- Spies are not perfect — detection is probabilistic, not guaranteed
- **False positives**: Sometimes when spies look for sedition too hard, they find things that are NOT there. This creates a "boy who cried wolf" dynamic — do you act on intelligence that might be wrong?
- Without surveillance infrastructure, a revolt can blindside the player completely

### Warning Signs (if surveillance is active)
- Unusual population behaviour patterns
- Unexplained resource discrepancies
- Communication intercepts
- Agent reports (which may be accurate, exaggerated, or fabricated)

## Food, Famine & Health

### Food Crises
- **Famine can kill population** — not just slow growth, but actual deaths
- **Plant disease** — crop failures affecting organics production
- **Parasite infestations** — buildings or population affected
- **Poor harvests** — random events reducing food output for a season
- **Engineered plagues** — biological weapons deployed by enemy spies

### Health System
Beyond just medical buildings, the player makes **active choices** during crises:

- **Aggressive triage** — Save people fast but condemn low-recovery-probability patients to death. Efficient, morally devastating. Ethical audit trail records this.
- **Hospital capacity overflow** — When hospitals are at capacity:
  - Enforce 16-hour shifts on medical staff to increase throughput
  - Build field hospitals (temporary, lower quality)
  - Ration medicine
  - Quarantine affected areas (slows spread but tanks happiness)
- **Pandemic response** — A spectrum from libertarian (let it run) to authoritarian (total lockdown)
- Each choice has trade-offs: speed vs. mortality vs. happiness vs. long-term consequences

### Spy-Engineered Plagues
- Enemy agents can engineer and deploy biological agents
- Detection requires counterespionage + medical research
- Particularly devastating if deployed before a military invasion
- The ethical implications of bioweapons should be reflected in the audit trail
