# Research Vision — Director's Notes

## Tiered Language Research (Per-Species)

Language research is NOT universal. Learning Khazari does not help you speak Sylvani.

### Three tiers per species:

1. **Xenolinguistics (Basic)** — Very basic commands: "Leave," "Stop," "Give [resource]." Enough to avoid accidental wars. Required before any meaningful interaction.
2. **Trade Language** — Sufficient for commerce, embassies, treaties. Enables trade routes and diplomatic agreements.
3. **Scientific Collaboration** — Full linguistic integration. Enables joint research projects, technology sharing, deep cultural exchange. This is where even fluent foreign speakers struggle — technical precision.

A generic "Xenolinguistics" foundation tech could give a small head start, but each species' language is its own research chain. This creates natural diplomatic bottlenecks — you might be able to trade with two species but can't even say "hello" to a third.

## Species-Specific Tech Trees

The long-term vision is **unique tech trees per species**. Currently building a universal tree that works as the foundation. Species-specific trees will branch from and modify the universal one.

- Kaelenth (ancient AI) shouldn't research "basic computing" — they START with it but lack "biology"
- Sylvani tech should feel botanical — growing technology rather than building it
- Orivani "tech" is faith-flavoured but functionally a tech tree (see below)
- Vaelori research through psychic resonance, not lab work

## Faith-Science Harmony (Orivani & Others)

Faith and science are **not opposed**. The Orivani's faith is based on something real:

- The sacred energy of stars
- Universe energies powered by the souls of the deceased
- Phenomena that are measurable but interpreted through spiritual language

**"They might not call it science, but it's still a tech tree and it's still their version of technology."**

This means:
- The Orivani tech tree has the same FUNCTION as any other (unlocks, bonuses, buildings)
- But the FLAVOUR is religious: "Revelation of the Star-Fire" instead of "Fusion Physics"
- Other species may also blend faith and science in different proportions
- The faith resource becomes a genuine input to research for faith-heavy species

## Devourer Reverse-Engineering

No dedicated post-victory tech branch. Instead, technology is unlocked through:

- **Battle salvage** — Wreckage from destroyed Devourer ships yields materials and components
- **Scraps** — Partial technology fragments that need assembly
- **Telemetry** — Sensor recordings from combat encounters
- **Combat recordings** — Tactical analysis of Devourer behaviour patterns

This is a **gradual process** — each battle yields more data. The player slowly deciphers Devourer technology, enabling:
- Better weapons (adapted from Devourer designs)
- Better shields (understanding their offensive capabilities)
- Better ships (structural innovations from salvage)
- The ability to **overwhelm them with numbers** using superior local production

The Devourer can't be matched 1:1, but their tech can be partially understood and countered.

## Xeno-Archaeology

A full mechanic, not just a tech:

- **Dig sites** on ancient worlds — multi-turn excavation projects
- **Artefact recovery** — physical objects that provide bonuses or unlock research
- **Discoveries include:**
  - Special abundant star system locations (resource-rich systems revealed on map)
  - Scientific advancements (tech unlocks or research speed bonuses)
  - Special building type unlocks (precursor buildings)
  - Lore fragments (pieces of the Devourer foreshadowing puzzle)
  - Warnings and messages from extinct civilisations
- Requires investment of time and effort — not free, not guaranteed
- Some artefacts may be dangerous or cursed
