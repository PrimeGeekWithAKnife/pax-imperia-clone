# AI Vision — Director's Notes

## AI Personality — D&D-Style Trait Profiles

Each AI species gets a personality profile built from discrete traits, like old-school D&D NPC personality tables. A profile like "inquisitive, driven, impulsive, fair" creates unique behaviour without scripting every scenario.

Reference: https://adnd2e.fandom.com/wiki/DMG_Table_70

### Trait Dimensions (derived from D&D tables, adapted for 4X)
Traits should cover axes like:
- **Honesty** — truthful ↔ deceitful
- **Bravery** — courageous ↔ cowardly
- **Ambition** — driven ↔ content
- **Temperament** — patient ↔ impulsive
- **Empathy** — compassionate ↔ cruel
- **Openness** — curious ↔ xenophobic
- **Loyalty** — steadfast ↔ treacherous
- **Pragmatism** — principled ↔ opportunistic

Each species gets a default personality profile, but individual AI empires can have random variation (a particularly cautious Khazari, an unusually aggressive Sylvani) to keep games unpredictable.

### Species Should Be True to Their Nature
- AI deception is **race-dependent**. A vicious, deceitful race should be played deceitfully to the core.
- Khazari: honour-bound, aggressive, direct. They tell you they're going to attack. Then they attack.
- Ashkari: mercantile, slippery, transactional. Every deal has a hidden angle.
- Nexari: logical, polite, relentless. They offer you assimilation with genuine warmth.
- Vethara: charming on the surface, unsettling underneath. Are they being diplomatic or infecting your diplomat?

## AI Difficulty — No Resource Cheating

**Resource cheating is explicitly rejected.** The AI does not get hidden bonuses.

Difficulty is determined by:
- **How ambitious the AI is** — how aggressively it pursues victory conditions
- **How competent its decision-making is** — better prioritisation, fewer mistakes
- **The personality mix of AI opponents** — some species are naturally less agreeable, more hostile, more competitive
- "Hard" difficulty means the AI plays with maximum ambition and competence, not with extra resources

The player feels challenged because the AI is *smart*, not because the game is rigged.

(Note: truly competent, competitive AI is extremely hard to build. This is the aspiration, understanding that iterative improvement is the path.)

## AI Diplomacy — True to Race

- AI deception capability is **determined by species personality**, not difficulty level
- An honest species will honour treaties. A treacherous species will break them when advantageous.
- AI should be capable of:
  - Signing treaties it intends to break (if the species is deceitful)
  - Feeding false intelligence
  - Cold war tactics without open conflict
  - False flag operations
  - Genuine, lasting alliances (if the species is loyal)
- The player should be able to learn species reputations: "The Khazari always honour their word. The Ashkari... less so."

## AI Cold War & Asymmetric Warfare

The AI should engage in the full spectrum of sub-war aggression:

- **Espionage** — Spy networks, agent operations
- **Cold war tactics** — Military posturing, arms races, proxy conflicts
- **False flag operations** — Attacks designed to look like someone else did it
- **Sabotage** — Targeting infrastructure, research, production
- **Hacking** — Digital/cyber warfare against systems and communications
- **Arson** — Destroying specific targets (buildings, crops, stockpiles)
- **Inciting unrest** — Funding dissidents, exploiting unhappy populations
- **Planting rumours** — Disinformation to damage reputation or trigger diplomatic crises
- **Asymmetric warfare** — Using unconventional tactics when conventionally outmatched

## AI Reaction to the Player

The AI should **consider the player's known actions** when making decisions:

- Has the player been honourable or deceitful?
- Has the player been hostile or peaceful?
- Does the player engage in cold war tactics?
- Has the player committed atrocities?
- What's the player's military strength relative to the AI?

This assessment drives AI behaviour: an AI species that knows the player is a treaty-breaker will never trust a peace offering. An AI that respects the player's strength will avoid provocation.

## AI Reaction to the Devourer

Post-victory behaviour:

- AI species are **generally pro-player** — the player achieved victory, and the Devourer threatens everyone. Self-preservation drives cooperation.
- **However**, some races may **remain hostile**:
  - Species the player genocided or subjugated may refuse to cooperate
  - Species with treacherous personalities may try to cut separate deals
  - Species with fatalistic or religious beliefs may accept destruction (Orivani: "The Coming is here. This is what we prayed for.")
- AI should contribute to the Emergency Council proportionally to their trust in the player
- AI species that the player defended gain loyalty; those left to fend for themselves lose it

## Open Questions
- How many personality traits should each AI profile have? (4-6 seems right)
- Should AI personalities be visible to the player, or discovered through interaction?
- How does AI handle the transition from "competing for victory" to "cooperating against the Devourer"?
- Should AI species be able to achieve victory conditions themselves, triggering the Devourer on THEIR victory?
- How does AI evaluate the player's ethical audit trail — do they track specific actions or just an aggregate score?
