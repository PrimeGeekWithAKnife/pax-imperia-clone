# Combat Vision — Director's Notes

## Combat Feel
- **Tactical and physics-based**. Length determined by how closely matched ships are.
- **Prolonged when closely matched.** Two equivalent fleets should slug it out.
- **Player direction and choices should make a decisive difference.** Tactics matter more than numbers alone.

## Tactical Layer
- Player CAN auto-resolve, but **auto-resolve must be earned**:
  - Must fight similar battles at least 10 times before auto-resolve produces reliable outcomes
  - Otherwise auto-resolve is unreliable (worse than manual)
- **Player tactical options:**
  - Choose formation
  - Position specific ships
  - Set stance per ship/group: aggressive, defensive, guard a specific ship
  - **Ram** — physical collision attack
  - **Hostile boarding** — capture enemy ships
  - **Firing line** — broadside formation, concentrated firepower
  - **Concentrate fire** — focus all weapons on a single target
  - **Skirmish** — keep maximum distance, "stick and move"
- **Ground offensives** — separate mechanics for planetary invasion/defence

## Scanning (Deep Space Probe)
- Scanning reveals stats for star systems and detects other races (unless cloaked)
- **Low-tech scanning** takes multiple turns
- **High-tech scanning** is instant
- Scanning should detect: system composition, planet types, resources, habitability, presence of other species/fleets

## Fleet Composition
- **Advantages, not hard counters.** A fleet of battleships would likely beat a mixed fleet UNLESS the battleships are overspecialised with a distinct weakness.
- Ship customisation matters: battleships can be massive missile boats OR precision energy beam platforms. The design choice creates vulnerabilities.
- Mixed fleets offer flexibility; mono-fleets offer raw power but risk being countered.

## Devourer Ships
- **Very alien, more advanced.** Should feel fundamentally different from player ships.
- **Smaller but far more powerful.** Their tiny ships equip like player mediums; their mediums like player capitals.
- They have superiority in: speed, shields, firepower, and tactics in most situations.
- The visual design should be unsettling — not evil-looking, just WRONG. Like something built by intelligence that doesn't think like us.
