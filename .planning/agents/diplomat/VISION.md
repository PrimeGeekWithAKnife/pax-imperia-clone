# Diplomacy Vision — Director's Notes

## First Contact

First contact is a **choice-driven event**, not automatic.

- Without Xenolinguistics, first contact is a gamble:
  - Send greetings → could be interpreted as friendly, strange, offensive, or warlike
  - Ignore them → they may ignore you back, or take offence
  - Open fire → immediate war, no ambiguity
  - The OTHER race reacts unpredictably: attack, flee, or total confusion
- **Without Xenolinguistics on at least one side, anything can happen**
- Once at least ONE of the two species has basic Xenolinguistics, basic communication is possible: "We are peaceful," "Leave this system," "Trade?"
- First contact outcomes should vary by species personality — Khazari might posture aggressively by default; Sylvani might be patient and wait; Ashkari might immediately try to sell you something

## Treaty Types

Full spectrum of diplomatic agreements:

| Treaty | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Non-Aggression Pact** | We won't attack each other. Cheapest, most basic. |
| **Trade Agreement** | Establish trade routes, share resources at negotiated rates. |
| **Research Sharing** | Pool research on specific projects, share discoveries. Requires Tier 3 language. |
| **Military Alliance** | Come to each other's defence if attacked by a third party. |
| **Vassalism** | One species becomes a vassal — autonomous but paying tribute and following foreign policy. |
| **Federation Membership** | Multiple species join a federation — shared governance, shared defence, shared economy. |
| **Subjugation** | "You will serve us or face destruction." Explicit enslavement ultimatum. Diplomatic consequences severe. |
| **Unification** | "We're such good mates we should unite under one banner — mine." The other species joins as a distinct population with their own modifiers. They retain identity but fall under your governance. |
| **Assimilation** | For species that can assimilate (Nexari, Vethara). Peaceful absorption — no war required. The other species' numbers bolster yours as they BECOME your species. |

### Key distinction: Unification vs. Assimilation
- **Unification**: they join but remain a distinct population. Their traits, culture, and modifiers persist within your empire. Internal politics emerge.
- **Assimilation**: they cease to exist as a separate species. Their population is converted. More efficient, more horrifying.

### Treaty breaking
- Treaties can be broken. Costs include:
  - Reputation damage (ethical audit trail)
  - Diplomatic penalties with ALL known species
  - Potential war with the betrayed party
  - Internal happiness penalty (your own people may disapprove)

## Espionage — Deep and Dangerous

Espionage is a full, deep system. Agents can:

- **Steal technology** — Acquire a tech the target has researched
- **Sabotage buildings** — Damage or destroy structures on enemy planets
- **Assassinate leaders** — Target planetary rulers, fleet admirals, other agents, governors
- **Incite rebellion** — Cause unrest, riots, civil war on enemy planets
- **Plant false intelligence** — Feed disinformation to mislead enemy decisions
- **Turn enemy spies** — Convert captured agents into double agents
- **Steal a ship** — Commandeer an enemy vessel (difficult, high-value)
- **Steal a planet** — The hardest, most time-consuming espionage operation. A planet secretly rebels against its rulers, joining YOUR empire. Requires years of groundwork, embedded agents, cultivated dissidents. The planet "flips" without a shot fired.

### Agent targets
Agents can be directed against specific targets:
- Planetary rulers / governors
- Fleet admirals / military leaders
- Other agents (counterespionage)
- Research directors
- Trade infrastructure

## The Vethara Question

The Vethara (symbiotic/parasitic species) create a unique diplomatic situation:

- **Some species view them as legitimate intelligent life** — a species that happens to need primitive hosts to function. Worthy of respect and diplomacy.
- **Others consider them a gross abomination** that should be destroyed on sight.
- Their origin may be significant — perhaps they came to be by **infecting a minor species**, which raises questions about consent and natural rights.
- As a diplomatic partner, they are **excellent at espionage** (literally inside your head) but their dependence on hosts creates practical limitations.
- Diplomatic opinion of the Vethara could be a major factional divider — species that accept them vs. species that want them eradicated.

## Post-Victory: The Emergency Council

When the Devourer arrives:

- An **Emergency Council** is proposed to all remaining species
- All surviving races **pool resources** to the player's victorious empire:
  - Two-thirds of their construction capacity
  - Two-thirds of their energy production
  - Two-thirds of their research output
  - Donated until the Devourer is defeated
- **This is conditional on the player defending everyone:**
  - If the player fails to defend allied species (or doesn't even attempt it), they may **vote to oust the player** as defender
  - Resource donations are withdrawn
  - The coalition fractures
  - Species may attempt to negotiate individually with the Devourer (futile but desperate)
- **The ethical audit trail matters enormously here:**
  - A player who built alliances has willing, generous partners
  - A player who conquered everyone has resentful, unreliable subjects
  - A player who committed genocide has no one left to call
