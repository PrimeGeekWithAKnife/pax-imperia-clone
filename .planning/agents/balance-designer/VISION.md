# Balance Vision — Director's Notes

## Game Length

- Pace scales with galaxy size naturally
- **Huge galaxy (1000 systems) may last 100 hours**
- Even with good tech, moving takes time — distance is real
- Small galaxies are quick; huge galaxies are marathons
- No artificial pacing — the geography and mechanics determine length

## Snowball Prevention

**No artificial rubber-banding.** No hidden penalties for being ahead.

- Running a successful empire is hard work enough
- Expansion brings its own problems: overextension, supply lines, multi-species management, unhappy populations, governor corruption, revolts
- Natural checks: larger empires are harder to defend, harder to keep happy, harder to govern
- The player earns their dominance or loses it through their own mistakes

## Devourer Fleet Sizes (by difficulty)

| Difficulty | Fleet Composition | Total Ships |
|---|---|---|
| **Easy** | 1 fleet, ~20 ships | ~20 |
| **Normal** | 1 fleet, ~50 ships | ~50 |
| **Hard** | 4 fleets in various locations, ~25 each | ~100 |

- Each Devourer ship is worth ~10 player ships
- To force retreat: destroy two-thirds of the expeditionary force
- Easy: destroy ~13 ships (equivalent to beating ~130 player ships' worth)
- Normal: destroy ~33 ships (~330 player ships' worth)
- Hard: destroy ~66 ships across 4 locations (~660 player ships' worth — requires galactic mobilisation)

Hard difficulty with 4 dispersed fleets means the player can't concentrate — they must defend multiple fronts simultaneously, making the Emergency Council essential.

## Early Game Pacing

- **Quiet exploration.** Start with one probe. Focus on research and planetary management.
- No military pressure at the start — but it comes naturally
- Especially if another race spawns in a neighbouring system
- Early game rhythm: scan systems → colonise → build economy → research → first contact
- The player should feel like an explorer, not a general, in the first hour
- First scout ship comes from research + shipyard construction — not immediate

## Victory Condition Timing

- **Violence is quick per battle, but conquest victory is long** — getting to a system to conquer it is a challenge in the early game. You need fleets, supply lines, enough force to take AND hold.
- **Diplomatic victory takes time** — trust is earned slowly
- **Research victory** — steady investment, no shortcuts
- **Economic victory** — accumulation over time
- No victory should be trivially faster than others. Each has its own bottleneck:
  - Conquest: logistics and force projection
  - Diplomatic: trust and language barriers
  - Research: education, scientists, facilities
  - Economic: trade routes, resource scarcity, infrastructure
