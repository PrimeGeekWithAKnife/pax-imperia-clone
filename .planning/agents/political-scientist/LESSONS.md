# Political Scientist -- Lessons Learnt

## Current State
- 14 government types already defined (democracy, republic, federation, autocracy, empire, theocracy, oligarchy, military_junta, technocracy, hive_mind, forced_labour, dictatorship, equality, tribal_council)
- Demographics engine tracks faith distributions (fanatics, observant, casual, indifferent, secular) and loyalty distributions (loyal, content, disgruntled, rebellious)
- Five government ages defined (Dawn, Expansion, Ascendancy, Dominion, Transcendence) with species-specific evolution paths
- Missing: dynamic factions, elections, voting, propaganda, corruption, revolutions, coups

## Director Decisions (29 March 2026)
- Hybrid faction model: species-specific starter factions + demographics-driven emergence
- 2-5 active factions per empire, dynamic -- factions form and dissolve
- Factions are ACTIVE agents with escalation ladder (lobby → fund → strike → coup)
- Election manipulation escalation: legitimate → grey area → clandestine (with increasing risk)
- Election results have real mechanical bite -- winning faction constrains player options
- Hive Mind ≠ Uni-Mind -- individuals that combine still have preferences/disagreements
- Revolutions preventable by addressing root causes (Maslow's Hierarchy framework)
- Player can instigate revolutions in own empire -- but losing means game-changing consequences
- Conquered planet rebellion is context-dependent, not automatic
- Clandestine "shadow buildings" -- white-labelled, require espionage to discover
- No objective truth in the game -- propaganda erodes relationship with reality, not a simple penalty
- Psionic/high-education species resistant to propaganda but may be better at wielding it
- 7 policy domains: foreign, domestic, economic, political, education, health, security
- Policy changes follow legislative pipeline: declaration → effect → enforcement → adoption
- Judicial system needed for policy enforcement
- Government type constrains extreme policies -- transition required first

## What to Avoid
- Stellaris factions: "static list of demands you only care about for unity" -- players hate this
- ES2 political volatility: elections dramatically shifting party composition felt random, not strategic
- ES2 shallow party differentiation: percentage tweaks, not fundamentally different governance
- ES2 passive laws: enact and forget, no ongoing decisions
