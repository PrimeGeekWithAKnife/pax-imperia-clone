# Economist -- Lessons Learnt

## Current State
- Economy engine handles 6 resource types (credits, minerals, rare elements, energy, organics, exotic materials)
- Building maintenance, fleet upkeep, tax rates, energy deficit effects all implemented
- Trade routes are physical paths through wormhole networks with establishment periods, maturity bonuses, disruption mechanics
- Waste engine exists (waste.ts)
- Missing: commodity marketplace, inflation/deflation, corruption scaling, corporate entities, loans/debt, stock market, economic warfare, luxury goods trading

## Director Decisions (29 March 2026)
- Two-tier market: local markets (early) → galactic reserve currency via council (mid-late)
- Local currencies devalue as council gains members -- Euro-model transition with conversion period
- 35 commodities: 20 common, 10 rare, 5 ultra-rare tied to age-specific tech/weapons
- All empires share the same markets -- market manipulation is intended gameplay
- Galactic Bank and Galactic Council are SEPARATE institutions with conflicting agendas
- Bank lends to both sides in a war; council wants peace -- institutional tension
- Corruption driven by root causes (inequality, poor laws, bureaucracy), tracked per planet/governor/admiral
- Full monetary policy toolkit: central bank, interest rates, austerity, yield curve control, taxation
- Inter-empire lending creates debt-trap diplomacy -- everything negotiable with rational self-interest
- True bankruptcy is catastrophic: defaults, riots, civil war, potential game-ending government collapse
- Corporations are fully autonomous entities, not player-controlled; can become vassal factions
- Cross-border corporate operations, tourism, industrial espionage all active
- Economic warfare is primarily clandestine -- shell companies, white-label merchants, shadow fleets
- Cuba model: sanctions weaken but cannot alone defeat (force multiplier, not victory condition)
- Piracy in three tiers: criminal enterprise, state-sponsored clandestine, act of desperation
- No objective proof = no diplomatic consequence -- plausible deniability is the game

## What to Avoid
- Stellaris galactic market: "instant buy anything" trivialises economics -- players criticise this heavily
- "Reaching a critical deficit is never a problem since anything can always be bought instantly" -- must avoid
- X4 Foundations opacity: great supply chains but players can't understand what's happening
