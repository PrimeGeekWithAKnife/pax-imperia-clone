# Economy Vision — Director's Notes

## Trade Routes — Physical and Vulnerable

Trade routes are **physical**. Ships fly between systems along defined paths.

- There is an obvious shortest/safest path between trading partners
- Routes **can be raided** — piracy, enemy fleets, Ashkari opportunism
- Routes **can be rerouted** — around war zones, dangerous systems, blockades
- Routes **can be protected** — escort fleets, patrol stances, defensive stations
- Disrupting an enemy's trade routes is a valid military strategy
- This creates geography-matters gameplay: chokepoint systems become strategically vital

## Currency & Trade

- Internal economy uses credits (or species-equivalent)
- External trade is **resource-based, not currency-based**
- **Some races do not have money.** Credits mean nothing to them. Trade must be in kind.
- Tradeable goods include:
  - Ships
  - Planets
  - Technology
  - Information / intelligence
  - Trade route access
  - **Population** (willing migrants, slaves, or hostage exchanges)
- Exchange rates emerge naturally from supply/demand and diplomatic relationships

## Resource Scarcity

- Some resources should be **very rare or very low supply**
- Regional resource distribution forces trade or conquest:
  - Exotic materials only near neutron stars
  - Rare elements concentrated in specific regions
  - Organics abundant in one quadrant, absent in another
- This creates natural economic interdependence between species
- Resource scarcity is a primary driver of conflict AND cooperation

## Black Market Mechanics

The black market is a **full mechanic**, not just an Ashkari building:

- **Smuggling** — Move contraband goods past blockades for premium profit
- **Contraband** — Certain goods are illegal under some governments (weapons, slaves, narcotics, forbidden tech)
- **Risk/reward** — More profitable than legal trade but risks diplomatic incidents
- **Intelligence gathering** — Black markets are information networks. Spies operate through them.
- **Government interaction:**
  - Some government types may **encourage or promote** black markets for unique benefits
  - Information gathering and espionage channels
  - Destabilising enemy economies
  - Others may crack down, creating underground resistance

## Post-Devourer Economics

When the Devourer harvests stars and hollows planets, the economy collapses. The player chooses how to react:

- **War economy** — All civilian production converted to military
- **Rationing** — Strict resource allocation, happiness penalty
- **Forced labour** — Maximum production at maximum moral cost
- **Complete militarisation** — Every citizen a soldier, every factory an arsenal
- **Desperate innovation** — Crash research programs, shortcuts, experimental weapons
- **Kamikaze measures** — Suicide ships, expendable crews, one-way missions
- **Suicidal soldiers** — Units that fight to the death with combat bonuses
- **Booby-trapped stars** — Rig stars to explode when the Devourer tries to harvest them
- **Booby-trapped ships** — Vessels designed to detonate inside Devourer formations

The full spectrum from measured response to scorched-earth desperation should be available. The ethical audit trail records which measures the player chose.
