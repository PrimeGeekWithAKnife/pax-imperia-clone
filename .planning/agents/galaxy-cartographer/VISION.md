# Galaxy Vision — Director's Notes

## Galaxy Edge & 3D

- There is no hard boundary — technology can reveal MORE
- Distant stars that on a 2D map appear beyond the "edge" may actually be "higher" or "lower" in 3D space
- **The galaxy view should be 3D** (or at least 2.5D with depth)
- Stars exist above and below the galactic plane
- Research unlocks the ability to detect and travel to distant outlier stars
- Intergalactic travel is theoretically possible with extreme tech — but the distances are staggering

## Wormholes

### Static & Dynamic
- Most wormholes are **static** — permanent connections between systems
- Some are **unstable** — they may (very rarely) appear spontaneously, creating new routes
- Spontaneous wormholes are rare events that reshape the strategic map

### Player Wormhole Technology
- **Wormhole collapse** — Research tech to destroy existing wormholes. Defensive tool — cut off enemy approach routes.
- **Mobile wormhole station** — Research tech to create a deployable wormhole generator. A space station that creates a new wormhole connection. Massive investment, game-changing strategic tool.
- **Sealed wormhole research** — Player can research to unseal wormholes that were deliberately closed. What's on the other side? Precursor space? Something worse?

## System Variety

Space should be **interesting to explore**. Not just star + planets.

### Celestial Wonders
- **Binary star systems** — Two stars, unusual orbital mechanics, exotic planets
- **Neutron star systems** — Rare elements, dangerous radiation, unique materials
- **Black hole systems** — Harvestable for exotic materials, extreme physics, time dilation effects?
- **Living stars** — Stars with anomalous behaviour, possibly sentient (Devourer foreshadowing?)
- **Dyson sphere ruins** — Precursor megastructure, partially intact. What powered it? What happened?
- **Super-massive earths** — Giant habitable worlds with enormous population capacity
- **Rogue planets** — Free-floating worlds between systems

### Anomalies & Phenomena
- **Odd space phenomena** — Nebulae with strange properties, gravity anomalies, subspace rifts
- **Rare minerals** — Resources found nowhere else, driving trade or conquest
- **Unique radiation effects** — Zones that boost or hinder specific technologies
- **Ancient ruins on planets** — Precursor dig sites, alien artefacts
- **Space debris fields** — Wreckage of ancient battles, salvageable technology
- **Signals** — Strange transmissions from unknown sources (Devourer foreshadowing)

## Minor Species Placement

### Distribution
- **Random scatter** across the galaxy
- All sorts of organisms: small animals, primitive civilisations, technically terrestrial creatures
- **Technology cap: nothing beyond roughly First World War advancement** — pre-spaceflight, pre-nuclear
- A great variety of weird and wonderful creatures
- **Multiple minor species can exist in one system** — even on the same planet (different continents)

### Variety
- **Aquatic minor species** — ocean-dwellers, natural affinity with Drakmari
- **Subterranean minor species** — tunnel-dwellers, natural affinity with Zorvathi
- **Aerial/floating species** — gas giant inhabitants?
- **Silicon-based primitives** — natural affinity with Pyrenth/Khazari
- **Hive organisms** — natural affinity with Zorvathi/Nexari
- Minor species should feel genuinely alien and diverse — not just "medieval humans on another planet"
